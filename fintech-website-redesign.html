FINTECH WEBSITE REDESIGN

B

When our client approached us to redesign a legacy fintech platform their employees had been using for over a decade, the scope and sensitivity of the effort was immediately clear. With a deeply embedded user base and a highly complex system built up over ten years, the redesign demanded more than a visual refresh — it required a thorough understanding of how employees actually worked.

I made employee research a constant throughout the process. From edge case interviews to personal pain point sessions, I immersed myself in each assigned section of the legacy platform, exploring it independently to understand every workflow and nuance before proposing changes. This ongoing dialogue with real users, combined with close collaboration with my senior design lead and product manager, led to the development of a foundational site skeleton that earned early client alignment and became the structural basis for the entire project.

From that skeleton, I went on to design approximately 300 screens over the course of a ten-month engagement. A central pillar of the redesign was an elevated AI experience — built to help employees surface the actions most relevant to their role and support call-centric workflows more fluidly. This reduced the cognitive effort required to navigate a historically data-heavy interface and allowed employees to move with greater confidence and speed.

The final platform struck a deliberate balance: modernized and customizable in feel, yet respectful of the workflows and mental models employees had developed over years with the legacy system. The result was a desktop experience that felt familiar enough to ease adoption while being meaningfully more efficient, digestible, and built for the demands of contemporary enterprise work.
